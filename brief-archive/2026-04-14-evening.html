The Hormuz tape just repriced that bet in real time. Forward call: if the blockade holds past April 24, expect RBI FX intervention, a CGD (city-gas-distribution) stock surge in Mumbai next week, and the first credible AI capex pullback guidance on the May earnings cycle.

Long Game · Worth Your Saturday
Gradual Disempowerment: 10 framings of a takeover you don’t notice

David Krueger’s new framework (“Gradual Disempowerment,” April 2026) is the most durable piece of AI-risk writing this quarter. Ten ways civilisations lose control of powerful systems without a single catastrophic event: institutional capture, information asymmetry, economic displacement cascades, regulatory lag, agent coordination externalities, etc. Ryan Greenblatt’s companion timeline argues the first three are visible in 2026 data already (OpenAI-Amazon detachment from MSFT, CHIPS Act capture, algorithmic labor markets).

Why it matters for you: most AI policy debates still assume a crisis-and-response loop. Krueger’s contribution is pointing out that the steady-state adjustment is the outcome — we’re already inside it. The best counter is not slower AI; it’s faster feedback-loop design. Read it cold; we’ll come back to it.
