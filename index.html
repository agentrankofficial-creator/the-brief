Editor's Read

Three transitions hit the wire inside twelve hours and every one of them is about succession — of leadership, of policy, of the hardware stack itself. Tim Cook is handing Apple to John Ternus on September 1, exactly the kind of engineer's handoff a company does when it has run out of showmanship and needs to ship chips. The US-Iran ceasefire expires today and nobody in Washington will say the word "renewal" out loud. And Mark Carney, with a fresh majority still warm in his pocket, did the first thing a Canadian prime minister has done in a generation that feels off-script — he suspended the federal fuel excise for 140 days. Not cut. Suspended. The difference is everything.

I am watching the India-Canada split right now the way one watches two rooms in the same house with different weather. Carney is pulling CAD 2.4 billion out of the federal till to make gasoline ten cents cheaper through Labour Day — a sovereign-independence gesture priced into every fill-up. Finshots this morning flags that India is doing the inverse: the cabotage relaxations for foreign carriers just got extended another six months, through October. Same Hormuz oil shock, two governments reading it in opposite directions. Ottawa relieves the consumer and picks a fight with fiscal orthodoxy. Delhi relieves the logistics network and picks a fight with domestic shippers. Both are correct for their own macros. Both leave a scar. The question for anyone operating in both countries — and Suhail, this is you — is which scar tissue is tradeable.

Underneath the politics, the Great CPU Famine is the story the market is not pricing yet. Agentic AI has quietly flipped the CPU-to-GPU ratio in new data-center builds from ~10:90 to closer to 50:50, Intel is supply-constrained into 2027, AMD took 3.6 points of server share YoY, and ARM just shipped its first in-house AGI CPU while NVIDIA announced Vera. The defensive x86 Ecosystem Advisory Group that Intel and AMD just co-founded is the 2026 equivalent of the Detroit OEM alliance of 2021, four years late. That is the Spotlight today because it is the one trade idea in the inbox that the Street hasn't crowded into — and it sits cleanly at the intersection of AI capex, sovereign compute, and the token-as-labor-unit thesis we planted a week ago.

AI INFRASTRUCTURE MARKETS ↑ CPU DEMAND ACT

The Great CPU Famine: Agentic AI just flipped the CPU-to-GPU ratio, and Intel is the bag-holder

Milk Road AI buried the most important infrastructure scoop of the week inside a tour of earnings calls: the CPU-to-GPU ratio in agentic AI workloads has shifted from roughly 10:90 toward 50:50. Every agent loop needs orchestration, memory management, tool-calling glue — all CPU-bound. Intel, still digesting the Gaudi-3 reset and the Tan-era restructure, is supply-constrained through at least H1 2027. AMD picked up 3.6 points of server CPU share YoY to 28.8%. ARM launched its first in-house AGI CPU last week. NVIDIA announced Vera, its own CPU, at GTC. Apple's M5 Ultra is already shipping to data-center partners under NDA.

The tell is defensive: Intel and AMD just co-founded the x86 Ecosystem Advisory Group — the industry equivalent of the Detroit Three announcing a joint EV consortium in 2021. When incumbents need a standards body, they've already lost the trajectory.

The trade: the consensus AI-capex trade is NVIDIA + hyperscaler. The non-consensus trade is ARM (infrastructure royalty, CPU seat at the table) and AMD (server share compounding) over a 12-month horizon, with Intel as the short leg. If the x86 Advisory Group ships only whitepapers by Q3, the famine becomes structural and ARM's multiple re-rates. See P153-3 below.

The Lead
CORPORATE AI → STRUCTURAL WATCH

Tim Cook hands Apple to John Ternus on September 1 — the engineer gets the keys during the CPU war

Apple confirmed overnight that Tim Cook, 65, will step down as CEO on September 1, 2026 and hand the role to John Ternus, currently SVP of Hardware Engineering and the executive responsible for Apple Silicon. Cook will remain as chairman. Ternus has been inside Apple since 2001, led the iPad team, and for the last three years has been the operational spine of the M-series and A-series chip roadmap. The succession was widely predicted by Apple analysts through 2025 but the timing — coming one quarter before the expected Anthropic IPO and inside the CPU famine described in our Spotlight — is not incidental.

Cook ran Apple as an operations and services company. Ternus will almost certainly re-tilt it back toward being a silicon company with consumer-device distribution. Expect the M5 Ultra data-center push (already shipping to partners under NDA per Milk Road AI) to accelerate, and expect the Vision Pro line to either get a silicon-first reboot or quietly retire. The under-discussed question is what Ternus does about AI services — Siri is still a liability, Apple Intelligence is under-delivering, and a chip guy is unlikely to solve that problem without an acquisition.

SO WHAT — INDIA + CANADA For India: Apple's India manufacturing trajectory (Foxconn/Tata) is a Ternus call now — operations-first would slow expansion; silicon-first could mean iPhone 18 and M-chip sub-assembly hitting Tamil Nadu faster. For Canada: AAPL is a top-5 weight in every Canadian pension and RRSP index fund; a succession-driven vol episode is a repricing event for CPP/OTPP overnight allocations.
ACTION AAPL is flat premarket. Don't chase today — see P153-1. The real trade is pairs: ARM/AMD long vs. AAPL/INTC short into the Sept 1 transition.
CONTRARIAN TAKE The Street is reading this as a continuity handoff. I think it is a strategy pivot dressed up as continuity. Ternus running Apple means Apple competes directly with NVIDIA on inference silicon within 18 months. That is a different company than the one Cook ran.

AI RESEARCH ↑ CAPABILITY WATCH

Anthropic's AARs paper — automating alignment research closes the PGR gap from 0.23 to 0.97

Jack Clark's Import AI #454 flags a paper from Anthropic: "Automating Alignment Research with AI Systems" (AARs). The core result: an AARs system solves alignment evaluation tasks at PGR (Percent Gap Recovered) of 0.97 versus a human researcher baseline of 0.23 on a private evaluation set. Translated: the system now does alignment research as well as a human researcher on this evaluation suite. Clark is careful to note the eval set is private and results are preliminary; the implication is that Anthropic's own alignment team is the first to be meaningfully displaced by its own models. If you're building an AI safety practice, the barrier-to-entry just collapsed.

CONTRARIAN TAKE 0.97 PGR on a private eval set is a reproducibility problem, not a capabilities claim. Wait for the external replication before calling this a breakthrough.

CANADA POLICY ↑ POLITICAL CAPITAL ACT

Carney suspends the federal fuel excise April 20 — September 7 — the sovereign-independence trade gets its policy wrapper

Mark Carney, with the majority barely 48 hours old, announced yesterday that the federal fuel excise tax is suspended from April 20 through September 7 (Labour Day). The suspension costs the treasury roughly CAD 2.4 billion, delivers an estimated 10¢/L reduction at the gasoline pump and 4¢/L on diesel, and — critically — is structured as a suspension rather than a permanent cut, preserving the instrument for reinstatement. This is the first concrete post-majority policy deliverable and it reads as a sovereign-independence gesture: Canada is independently absorbing the Hormuz oil shock rather than waiting for US policy cues.

SO WHAT — INDIA + CANADA India's Finance Ministry has been testing the excise-cut template for six weeks. Finshots this morning shows India chose a different lever — cabotage extension — to absorb the same shock. The Carney model (temporary suspension, not permanent cut) is the one India should copy to preserve fiscal optionality. See P153-4.
ACTION Canadian refiners (Parkland, Suncor downstream) see margin expansion inside the suspension window. Suncor is the cleaner play given its integrated upstream; Parkland is the retail-spread play.

The Long Game
AI · STRATEGY

The x86 Ecosystem Advisory Group is the 2026 equivalent of the Detroit auto-OEM alliance of 2021

When incumbents form a standards body, they have already conceded the trajectory. Intel + AMD + major OEMs just announced the x86 Ecosystem Advisory Group in the same week ARM shipped its first in-house AGI CPU and NVIDIA announced Vera. The historical precedent is precise: Detroit formed joint EV battery and software working groups in 2021 exactly as Tesla's architectural lead became structurally unrecoverable. The x86 group has until Q3 2026 to produce a concrete technical deliverable — a cross-vendor ISA extension, a chiplet compatibility standard, a unified accelerator spec. If they produce only whitepapers and governance docs, the CPU transition is decided and ARM's multiple re-rates by 35–50% over 24 months. See P153-3.
