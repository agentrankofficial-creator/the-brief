The Strait is Closed. Markets Are Betting It Won't Stay That Way.

The US Navy began a formal blockade of ships entering or exiting Iran through the Strait of Hormuz on April 13, following the collapse of 21 hours of negotiations in Islamabad personally led by Vice President JD Vance. The conflict is now in Day 46. Approximately 400 million barrels of oil that would have been produced in 2026 have been lost — a figure that could surpass 1 billion barrels if the conflict extends. Brent briefly topped $100/bbl during peak panic; WTI trades at $92.28 today. Yet equity markets are rising: the S&P is approaching all-time highs, Nifty is up 1.56%, TSX up 0.66%. The divergence tells you everything. Markets are pricing a deal, not a war.

SO WHAT — India: India imports ~85% of its oil. Every $1/bbl increase in crude costs India approximately $1.5B annually in extra import bills. With WTI at $92 versus pre-conflict ~$70, India is paying a ~$33B annual oil premium. Rupee remains under pressure; RBI will be watching closely. The Iran nuclear deal resolution is existentially important for India's import bill.

SO WHAT — Canada: Canada is a net oil exporter. Elevated WTI is a revenue tailwind for oil sands producers (Suncor, CNQ, Cenovus) and the Alberta government. But Carney just cut the gas tax to offset consumer pump pain — because refined petroleum price transmission hits consumers regardless of upstream producer gains.
ACTION: Watch the White House for a second Islamabad round — Trump told the NY Post it "could happen in the next two days." Iran signalling openness to nuclear talks is the critical shift from last week. Position for deal-announcement rally: energy longs can be trimmed, risk assets (India, emerging markets, BTC) benefit from Hormuz re-opening. Canadian oil sands benefit from sustained high prices if deal is delayed.
💭 Bear case: the 15-point US demand list (20-year enrichment moratorium, Hezbollah dissolution, Israel recognition) may be structurally impossible for any Iranian government to accept domestically. Hezbollah's rejection of Israeli talks signals proxy network is not cooperating. A prolonged conflict — 6-9 more months — would push the oil supply deficit past 1B barrels and trigger stagflationary pressure that no central bank can resolve cleanly.

The Deep View reports that helium supply disruption from the Iran conflict is already impacting AI data center component supply chains.
