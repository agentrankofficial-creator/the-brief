Alberta annexation YouTube campaign crosses 40M views — first documented AI-driven separatist op in a G7

The Media Ecosystem Observatory at McGill published a report Tuesday identifying 847 YouTube channels, most created in the last 90 days, pushing AI-generated content arguing Alberta should join the United States. Combined views exceed 40 million. Roughly 60% of the upload pattern matches known Russian and Chinese influence-operation fingerprints; 40% appears domestic-organic. This is the first time a G7 democracy has documented a synthetic-media separatist campaign at this scale, and it lands while Carney's majority government is still finalizing its throne speech. The response — likely a Digital Sovereignty Act combining platform transparency rules with an AI content-provenance mandate — will set the template for every Western democracy facing similar operations over the next 24 months. Nobody covered this beyond one Globe story and one Peak brief. Watch for the Bill C-number by May 15.

The Lead

SpaceX to acquire Cursor for $60B — largest AI M&A ever, Musk buys the developer choke point

The deal, leaked by The Information Tuesday night and confirmed by two sources to Bloomberg, values Cursor at roughly 150x forward revenue — a multiple last seen in late-2020 SaaS. It is the biggest AI acquisition by any measure and the first time a rocket company has owned a code editor. Musk's logic, per people close to him: every satellite, every Grok fine-tune, every Tesla autopilot patch now flows through an AI-assisted IDE. Owning that layer is owning the compounding flywheel of xAI and Tesla FSD simultaneously. The regulatory read is murkier. FTC Chair Ferguson has not signaled, but Senator Warren's office is already drafting a letter arguing the deal concentrates AI infrastructure in one person's hands alongside rockets, EVs, and social media. Microsoft, Google, Amazon — all GitHub Copilot/Gemini Code/CodeWhisperer operators — get hit asymmetrically if Cursor becomes xAI-native. Anthropic and Cognition (Devin) get a tailwind as the obvious neutral alternatives. Indian and Canadian operators using Cursor at scale should expect (a) a 60-day migration off if they compete with any Musk property, and (b) pricing resets within 6 months.

Why it matters: This is the moment AI developer tooling stopped being a SaaS category and became strategic infrastructure. Every CTO needs a diversification plan by Q3.

Policy · US

Tillis freezes Warsh Fed chair confirmation over "dovish inconsistencies"

Senator Thom Tillis (R-NC) placed a procedural hold on Kevin Warsh's confirmation Tuesday, citing Warsh's 2019 op-eds that endorsed negative real rates. Leader Thune says the vote slips past the April recess; market-implied probability of confirmation by June fell from 78% to 51% overnight. 10Y UST +4bps on the news.

The Tillis hold is unusual because Warsh was once a Tillis favorite — the senator cited him approvingly in 2019. The reversal reflects three things. First, the MAGA base wants an overt tight-money signal to weaken Powell's legacy, and Warsh's dovish papers complicate that narrative. Second, Tillis is in a tough 2026 primary and needs a right-flank win. Third, Treasury Secretary Bessent quietly prefers Hassett, and a Warsh delay opens that door.

The market impact is nonlinear. Every week Warsh slips, the 10Y UST adds 2-4bps as uncertainty premium compounds. If this drags past Memorial Day, Bessent almost certainly pivots to a Hassett nomination, which the market would read as more hawkish and push 10Y toward 4.5%. For Canadian and Indian operators: expect USD/INR to test 94.00 and CAD/USD to break 0.73 if this drags.

Watch: Thune whip count Monday · Bessent on CNBC Wednesday · Warsh FT op-ed expected Friday

Apple names John Ternus de facto successor to Tim Cook — succession signal strongest in 15 years

Bloomberg's Mark Gurman reports Apple's board formally designated hardware chief John Ternus as heir apparent at Tuesday's strategic review. No timeline, but insiders peg transition to FY2027. Ternus, 50, ran iPhone, iPad, Mac, and now oversees Vision Pro 2 and Apple Intelligence silicon.

This is the clearest Apple succession signal since Cook himself was tapped in 2011. Ternus over services chief Eddy Cue and SVP Greg Joswiak reads as Apple doubling down on hardware-as-moat in an AI era where software differentiation is collapsing. The choice also signals Apple Intelligence stays on-device, not cloud — Ternus built the silicon.

For India operators: expect a Ternus-era Apple to triple India manufacturing by 2029 and potentially announce a Bangalore silicon design center. For Canada: less direct impact, but Shopify's AI roadmap suddenly has fewer neutral allies as xAI eats Cursor and Apple doubles down internal.

Geopolitics · India-Korea

India–South Korea sign $8B semiconductor FTA supplement — first chip-specific bilateral

Minister Goyal and Korea's Ahn Duk-geun signed a chip-specific supplement to the 2010 CEPA in Seoul Tuesday, covering co-investment in fab construction, rare-earth supply swaps, and visa fast-tracking for 5,000 Korean engineers to India. Targets Samsung's planned Tamil Nadu fab and SK Hynix's HBM partnership with Micron-India.

This is India's second major chip-specific bilateral after the 2024 Taiwan deal, and the first that includes explicit rare-earth swaps — a direct hedge against China's export controls. The 5,000-engineer visa lane is significant; India's chip design talent pool needs 20,000 specialists by 2028 and Korean transfer learning compresses that timeline by 18 months. Expect a similar Japan bilateral by September.

For Canadian operators: watch if this template gets ported to the CEPA-2 India-Canada talks. Trade Minister Ng has been vocal about rare-earth coupling.

Canada · Policy

Carney throne speech delayed to May 20 — Digital Sovereignty Act in drafting

PMO confirmed Tuesday the throne speech slips to May 20 (from May 6) to incorporate a "comprehensive digital sovereignty framework" — PMO sources say it will combine platform transparency rules, AI provenance mandates, and foreign-funded political ad disclosure. With the Liberals now holding a working majority (post-election floor-crossing converted the April 2025 minority into a stable majority), passage is not in serious doubt; the editorial fight is over scope — Champagne pushing a harder provenance mandate, LeBlanc wanting narrower foreign-funded ad disclosure to avoid civil-liberties backlash. Opposition whip counts still matter on specific amendments in committee.

The delay is a direct response to the Alberta YouTube campaign. PMO wants to arrive with a coherent package rather than a defensive press release. Two things to watch. First, whether Champagne (Innovation) and LeBlanc (Public Safety) end up sharing the file — shared authority tends to slow enforcement. Second, whether Carney uses this to greenlight a broader "Buy Canadian Cloud" procurement rule that could affect AWS/Azure/GCP market share in federal contracts.

For operators: if you sell SaaS into the Canadian federal government, flag any AI provenance or data-residency capabilities in your Q2 proposals. This will become table-stakes by fall.
